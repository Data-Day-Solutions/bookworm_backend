Title: To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee:
Set in the American South during the 1930s, this novel follows Scout Finch as she witnesses her father, Atticus Finch, a lawyer defending a Black man wrongly accused of rape, exposing the injustices of racism and prejudice in their community.

Title: The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald:
Narrated by Nick Carraway, the story revolves around the mysterious millionaire Jay Gatsby and his obsessive love for Daisy Buchanan, highlighting the themes of the American Dream, wealth, and the destructive nature of the past.

Title: Pride and Prejudice by Jane Austen:
This classic romance follows Elizabeth Bennet, a witty and independent young woman, and her complicated relationship with the proud and wealthy Mr. Darcy, as they navigate social expectations and misunderstandings in 19th-century England.

Title: 1984 by George Orwell:
Set in a dystopian future, Winston Smith struggles against the oppressive regime of Big Brother, where privacy is nonexistent, and thought control is enforced through surveillance and manipulation.

Title: The Catcher in the Rye by J.D. Salinger:
Through the eyes of Holden Caulfield, a disillusioned teenager, the novel explores themes of alienation, loss of innocence, and the search for meaning in a hypocritical adult world.

Title: The Lord of the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien:
A sprawling fantasy epic following the hobbit Frodo Baggins as he embarks on a perilous quest to destroy the One Ring, an artifact of immense power, in the face of the Dark Lord Sauron's forces.

Title: The Handmaid's Tale by Margaret Atwood:
Set in a totalitarian theocracy, Offred, a fertile woman forced into servitude as a "Handmaid," narrates her experiences under a regime that has stripped women of their rights and autonomy.

Title: The Grapes of Wrath by John Steinbeck:
During the Dust Bowl era, the Joad family, displaced from their Oklahoma farm by drought and economic hardship, embarks on a difficult journey to California in search of a better life, encountering exploitation and struggle along the way.

Title: The Color Purple by Alice Walker:
Through a series of letters, this novel explores the lives of African American women in the rural South, focusing on the themes of female empowerment, sexual abuse, and the resilience of the human spirit.

Title: The Time Traveler's Wife by Audrey Niffenegger:
A love story between a time traveler, Henry, and his wife, Clare, navigating the complexities of a relationship where Henry is frequently displaced through time, causing disruptions and heartbreak.

Title: A Madeup Book by Dave Shaw:
This book is a compelling and intriguing mystery, which recounts the perilous journey of data scientist, and AI engineer, Dr. Dave as
he attempts to navigate his way through the ever-changing world of modern AI technologies and keeping up with Microsoft Certifications. This book is a romance thriller.
